Mar 09 2018: BVU Squad In Turmoil In Conf Tourney - by HeimTime21 on March 31st, 2021

Buena Vista University's one man scoring machine, Chad Jacobs is a shocking discovery on the injury report for tomorrow. Chad did not appear to get hurt last game which led to questions. BVU, who had legitimate conference championship hopes have a date with Ohio Wesleyan tomorrow night.

President HeimTime21 had a reported meeting with the Beaver's star player and was overheard leaving the dormitory while on the phone with what was presumed to be the head coach saying: "He said he's sitting out the rest of the conference tournament for fatigue.... Yeah, like he's tired or some crap.... I'm not sure what we can do because he's our only hope in the D3 playoffs... I get it, kids these day are soft."

When Jacobs was asked about fatigue he smiled and said: "You'd be tired too carrying this squad on my back all season. My goal isn't to win some stupid little conference tournament. My goal is to win the D3 tournament. I don't see anything wrong with wanting a little time off to recharge my batteries before I lead us to the promised land. I talked to coach, he understands."

When we asked coach Gray about his star player's "injury" he simply stated: "I don't understand what he's thinking."

We'll see how this all shakes down tomorrow as our BVU Beavers take on the solid squad of Ohio Wesleyan.


Feb 17 2018: BVU Head Coach Hank Gray In Hot Water - by HeimTime21 on March 25th, 2021

Head coach Hank Gray is in hot water after last night's pivotal conference game against District of Columbia when he left the arena immediately after the game to meet the referees in the parking lot and have a loud altercation with them. Local DC police had no comment on the matter.

Hank Gray publicly apologized for his behavior after the game. "I should not have chased down the refs in the parking lot, they were awful the whole game and I should have lost it during the game and got ejected. Bobby Knight would be rolling over in his grave if he saw what these clowns did to me. What they did was wrong and there should be an investigation into their fraud."

Coach Gray's team had a solid performance during the game. They took more shots than DC, they shot a higher percentage than DC, they outrebounded them by 17. But the massive outlier were personal fouls. BVU had 19 personal fouls which led to 29 free throws. DC hit an incredible 83% from the line and had a very balanced scoring attack to win by 8.

BVU is hoping for a rubber match against the extremely solid DC squad. And is hoping that Jacobs can carry the team to a conference title in the coming weeks. It should be a fun sprint to the finish.
