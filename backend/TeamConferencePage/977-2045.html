Jan 05 2036: Mariners sink at Lake Superior - by mavstar21 on December 16th, 2023

Yellow Jacket fans were rejoicing after defeating old rival Mitchell last night by 10 points, with Craig Dobbins getting it done at both ends of the floor.

Things got spicy in the post match press conference however, when Mariners coach Garry Dobson asked why he spotted Yellow Jackets coach Harry Mancuso handing referee Ray Chamberlain a thick envelope after a game which had a lopsided 27-10 foul count. "Not at all - that was just me handing out the last of this seasons' Christmas cards" Mancuso responded.

Chamberlain was later spotted at the Superior Toyota dealership putting down a cash deposit on a new Corolla.


Mar 06 2031: Rule appeal incoming?? - by mavstar21 on March 28th, 2023

After going down to Mitchell in the first round of the Conference tourney, Wisconsin-Superior is considering an appeal to the HBICBRE (Hardwood Board of Imaginary College Basketball Rule Enforcement) to remove academic requirements for scholarship players after their season was derailed due to a combined 8 game pair of injuries to star Point Guard Ricky Bingham. Bingham sustained a shoulder injury reaching for a book on the top shelf in the library whilst cramming for exams in an attempt to raise his grade average. Coach Mancuso was overheard muttering, 'he should have just used chat GPT...that is how I do all my game planning'.

With Bingham out, the Yellow-Jackets pulled FR Billy Pennington out of his dorm room where he had been playing table soccer for the last two months straight, and he duly delivered a classy 6 assist/7 turnover game and was a witches hat on defence, letting Frank Buckley drop in a career best 8 three pointers.

Following the loss to the Mariners, Coach Mancuso took the team to Lake Inferior (Actually just the dam out the back of his house) for a bonding session, with frothies & a barbie, but only served up the home brand snags, saying - gourmet sausages are reserved for winners!
