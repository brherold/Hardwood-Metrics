Mar 25 2004: 2004 Season - by Balbinjj on February 12th, 2019

Big 3
PF: Douglas Young (173)
SG: Grady Butcher (152)
C: Devin Beck (119)

The team entered the season with a lot of promise because they have one of the most talented rosters in their conference.

They met the hype by having the 3rd best record in the league, punching their ticket to League 4.

They combined a steady diet of inside scoring with pressure defense and stable man-on-man defense.

Douglas Young was able to lock-in himself as the first ever hall-of-fame of the school after a stellar four years career.


Oct 03 2002: 2002 Season - by Balbinjj on October 29th, 2018


Big 3
SG: Stan Greco (153) JR
PG: Lonnie Cotton (149) JR
PF: Douglas Young (146) SO

The team entered the season full of aspiration of reaching the top 3.

They did not disappoint with their 8-2 start, predicated on their high-pressure defense.

Unfortunately, injuries and line-up decisions catch-up to them as they only managed 9-11 in their last 20 games.

Fortunately, their Big 3 of Stan Greco, Douglas Young and Lonnie Cotton will still be with them in the upcoming season.



Mar 10 2001: 2001 Season - by Balbinjj on August 24th, 2018

Big 3
SF: Enrique Adame (160) SR
PF: Neil Hook (149) SR
SG: Terry Quentin (147) SR

The inexperience of the management and the coaching staff led to a disastrous season for the Lancers. The management decided to take out the plug for the season before they even reached the halfway point.

They never found a way of answering their weakness inside. They failed also to maximize their big 3 who struggled all throughout the season.

Rebounding was a struggle all throughout the season as we gave up the biggest difference in rebounds. We also gave up the most Points in the Paint.

The brightest spot was out three-point shooting, wherein we ended up as the 2nd most efficient team.

The management is now looking forward to the next conference, anticipating a much weaker conference.
