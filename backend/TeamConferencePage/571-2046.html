Mar 26 2043: Official announcement: Augusta Jaguars 2043-For the Future. - by FatalityMCO on February 3rd, 2025

Even celebrating one of the best seasons in college history (37W-8L), a new era begins for the Augusta Jaguars. The hiring of a new Head Coach: Stan Beerd, comes along with the change in thinking of the College's management.

A new recruiting style is being adopted, a new scholarship policy and a new way of playing will also be worked on with the new Head Coach.

The search for players coming from High School is now being guided not only by hype or stars that each player has in the scout, it is being worked on how the player fits into the system, the arrival of 7 new players this season demonstrates this.

Augusta wants to reach the highest national divisions and to do so is trying to adapt its basketball to the most modern tactically.


Oct 12 2024: New Season at the Jaguars of Augusta - by FatalityMCO on March 16th, 2022

With the conference and division falling, it became clear that changes had to be made, so the team made the switch to the Head Coach, saying goodbye to Dejuan Biddle and hiring Eric Tidwell.

Another change was in the board, which has been much more aggressive in recruiting new talent, as the season passed without any quality player coming from the high school or international talent system.

The new season is already doing well with 3 wins in 3 exhibition games.

Starting with a victory by 10 points against Clemeson of South Carolina away home, the team started badly in the first half, losing by 14 points (34-48), but changes made by the new Head Coach, the team scored a sprint that led the team to an impressive victory by 24 points in the second half (58-34) the team was led by the 24 points, 8 rebounds and 5 steals of shirt 24 Marvin Hines.

The second game again away from home against the traditional UCF Knights team from Orlando, Florida from Division 1. The game started with scores from both teams, but at the end of the half UCF led by 7 points (30-37). Coach Tidwell made changes in the way of play that had an effect and the team was more competitive touching the scoreboard and reaching the front with 3:55 left to finish with a ball of #24 M. Hines that still kicked two free throws and put 3 points of advantage, 2:22 to go, the team opened 7 of advantage with Shirt 24 again making the basket and reaching the 80th point of the team, UCF still fought until the end and even left the difference in 1 point, but the victory was ours . Second Half (50-42). Again #24 Marvin Hines led the team with 27 Points and 6 Rebounds. another highlight was shirt #5 Owen Fraley who in 25 minutes scored 24 points with 4-8 on the 3 ball.

In the third game it was a protocol victory by 42 points. The team played well the entire game and practically every player stood out. the team was led by shirt #5 Owen Fraley who had 31 Points in 7-11 on the ball of 3. and #24 Marvin Hines who again had a good game with 19 Points and 15 Rebounds and finally shirt #54 Lincoln Roberts 18 Points and 11 Rebounds.
